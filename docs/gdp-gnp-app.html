GDP (Like you’re a child)

GDP is like counting everything the country makes inside its borders in a year.

GDP = “Total value of things made inside the country”
  • Car made in Lahore ✅ counts in Pakistan’s GDP
  • Pakistani worker makes a car in Dubai ❌ not in Pakistan’s GDP
Domestic = inside the country. GDP cares about where production happens.

GNP / GNI (Simple idea)

GNP (or GNI) counts income earned by a country’s residents, even if they earn it abroad.

GNP = GDP + (income residents earn abroad − income foreigners earn here)
  • Pakistani doctor in the UK ✅ adds to Pakistan’s GNP/GNI
  • Foreign company profits sent abroad ❌ reduces Pakistan’s GNP/GNI

Key Difference

GDP: Where? Inside borders (territory)
GNP/GNI: Who? Residents’ income (nationality/residency)

Two Equations to Remember

These are the ones your slides use.

GDP = C + I + G + (X − M)

C=Consumption, I=Investment, G=Government spending, X=Exports, M=Imports.

GNP/GNI = GDP + NFIA

NFIA = Net Factor Income from Abroad = (earned abroad) − (paid to foreigners).

Why GDP can “lie”

  • GDP can rise but poverty may still rise (distribution).
  • Home work (cooking/childcare) not counted.
  • Pollution damage not subtracted.
  • Informal economy may be missed.
Tip for class: Use GDP to measure production, not happiness.

What to emphasize in class

  • GDP is about production inside borders.
  • GNP/GNI is about residents’ income (includes abroad).
  • Nominal vs Real GDP matters for welfare.
  • Informal economy and distribution are why GDP can mislead.
Mini activity: Ask students: “If a Pakistani earns abroad, does GDP change?” → No. GNI does.
